House of Courts

Residential / Bangalore

Location
Bangalore, Karnataka
Area
4,500 Sq. Ft.
Principal Architect
Arjun Walia
Year
2025

The House of Courts is designed as a dialogue between the built form and the natural environment, creating a sanctuary within the urban density of Bangalore.

Conceptually, the home is organized around a series of internal courtyards that act as the lungs of the house. These voids allow light and air to penetrate deep into the floor plate while maintaining privacy from the street. The material palette is restrained, featuring exposed concrete, natural timber, and Kota stone, which grounds the structure in its context.

The transition between public and private spaces is modulated through levels and landscape buffers. The living areas open up completely to the central court, blurring the boundaries between inside and outside—a core tenet of our tropical modernist philosophy.
